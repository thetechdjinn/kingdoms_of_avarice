# Currency Plan

## Overview

Currency will consist of multiple types of currency. Each a multiple of the previous type.

1. Copper: 10 copper = 1 silver
2. Silver: 10 silver = 1 gold
3. Gold: 10 gold = 1 platinum
4. Platimum: 100 platinum = 1 Runic
5. Runic: There are no larger forms of currency.

**NOTE:** The primary reason for the runic's high multiple value is to be able to consolidate a lot of coins (therefore weight) into a single item.

**NOTE:** "runic" is both singular and plural.

## Runic

Runic is the highest form of currency.

### The Name Runic

The term "Runic" should be the default name for this type of currency, but it should be changeable by the game administrator in
the game settings.

### Currency as Items

Currency should be able to be picked up and dropped like any other item, but it should not be treated the same way. When displaying
your inventory, it should be displayed separately in the following form:

```
You have x runic, x platinum, x gold, x silver, and x copper.
Wealth: X copper farthings.
```

Where wealth is all the currency you have combined into a single value as copper farthings.

Example:

```
You have: 93 runic, 53 platinum, 15 gold, 5 silver, and 3 copper.
Wealth: 93531553 copper farthings.
```

The "You have:" and "Wealth:" should be in green, and the values should be in blue.

### Spending and Converting Coins:

Since you can end up collecting a lot of lower value coins. You should be able to convert them to higher value coins to reduce the amount of coins you are carrying and thereby reduce the weight of your inventory.

1. Purchasing or selling something in a shop should convert all the coins to the highest value coin.
2. Depositing coins into a bank and then withdrawing them again should convert all the coins to the highest value coin.
3. Training to the next level costs money. This transaction should convert all the coins to the highest value coin.

**NOTE:** Training (paying to train) to the next level does not yet exist. This is just a plan for the future.

**NOTE:** Banks and shops do not yet exist. This is just a plan for the future.

### Coin Weights

Coins weight should be dependent on element they are made of.

1. Copper: 25 copper = 1 encumbrance point
2. Silver: 25 silver = 1 encumbrance point
3. Gold: 15 gold = 1 encumbrance point
4. Platimum: 10 platinum = 1 encumbrance point
5. Runic: 4 runic = 1 encumbrance point

**NOTE:** The encumbrance point is the amount of weight that a character can carry before they become encumbered. Encumberance is already a feature of the game.

### Coin Descriptions

Below is the coins descriptions. While they have extended names in the description. Generally within the game they should be referred to by their short name. (copper, silver, gold, platinum, runic)

1. Copper: The copper farthings look like they've been around forever.
2. Silver: The silver nobles glitter with use.
3. Gold: The gold crowns are rustic and used.
4. Platimum: The platinum pieces shine as though they were new.
5. Runic: The runic coins glitter like nothing you have ever seen before.

### Thievery

Thievery should be able to be used to steal coins from players and NPCs. Though players cannot choose what they steal. It will be a random selection of what items are in the characters inventory (not worn) and then the item is stolen, or a random amount of coins of a specific type. (steal 23 silver coins, or steal 51 gold coins, etc)

**NOTE:** Thievery does not yet exist. This is just a plan for the future.
**NOTE:** There are no literal pouches or bags to store coins in the game, it is just assumed and doesn't impact thievery of coins.
